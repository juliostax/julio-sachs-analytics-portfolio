Case Study 1: Puroast Amazon Revenue & Fulfillment Restructuring
Problem

Puroast Coffee faced major pressure from rising green coffee costs, changing Amazon marketplace dynamics, fulfillment constraints, and the need to preserve cash flow while maintaining revenue.

Approach

I helped restructure the company’s Amazon infrastructure from a primarily 1P / purchase-order-driven model toward a more flexible 3P fulfillment system. This required analysis across revenue per pound, inventory needs, cash flow, fulfillment timing, production staffing, pricing, and channel economics.

Key work included:

Amazon revenue and unit-economics analysis
1P vs 3P fulfillment transition planning
Inventory and cash-flow modeling
Production scheduling and daily fulfillment planning
Labor-requirement analysis
Subscription and reorder-rate analysis
Marketplace performance tracking
Result
Increased Amazon revenue per pound sold from approximately $13/lb to $22/lb
Helped maintain overall revenue while improving unit economics
Helped the business withstand an approximately 150% increase in green coffee costs during 2024–2025
Reduced required production staffing by approximately 50%
Supported financial modeling around subscription revenue, reorder behavior, gross margin, EBITDA, and patent-backed financing

Marketplace analytics, financial modeling, revenue analysis, operations analytics, inventory planning, cash-flow forecasting, fulfillment optimization, executive reporting.

Case Study 2: Algorithmic MLB Betting Model
Problem

The goal was to identify late-market MLB underdog mispricing using a model trained around lines available close to game time, rather than relying on early-market movement or opening-line closing value.

Approach

I built a Python-based MLB betting simulation framework using probability estimation, expected-value logic, market-line comparison, and real-world results tracking.

The model focused on:

Late-market lines within roughly one hour of game start
Underdog-only market inefficiencies
Expected-value decision logic
Stake sizing and bankroll exposure
Partial-season performance tracking
ROI, net profit, sample size, and variance analysis
Result

Tracked partial-season Betstamp performance:

227 MLB wagers
Approximately $15.5K wagered
+$2,601.62 net profit
16.7% ROI
Average bet size approximately $67.80
Net profit equal to approximately 38x average bet size
Limitations

This was a partial-season tracked sample due to third-party app tracking limitations. The model was not designed primarily to maximize traditional CLV because it was trained and evaluated around late-market lines. Results should be interpreted as evidence of applied modeling skill and promising edge detection, not as a complete multi-year proof of persistent market alpha.

Skills Demonstrated

Python modeling, probability, simulation, expected value, performance tracking, market-price comparison, risk awareness, variance analysis.

Case Study 3: Crypto Strategy Dashboard & Dynamic Capital Allocation
Problem

The goal was to build a trading-strategy research dashboard that could evaluate crypto-market opportunities across different yearly regimes, rather than assuming every year had the same number of tradable opportunities.

Approach

I built a crypto strategy dashboard using historical simulation, market-data analysis, model scoring, dynamic allocation logic, and regime detection.

The system evaluates:

Eligible coins
Meta-strategy cumulative scores
HIGH vs LOW opportunity regimes
Dynamic capital allocation
NAV, peak NAV, and drawdown
Trade count and average holding period
Bootstrap events
Active deployed capital versus idle preserved capital

In HIGH-mode years, the model allows broader deployment across up to 30 active coins. In LOW-mode years, the model restricts active deployment to a small number of coins and reduces active capital exposure, preserving the remaining allocation for alternative use.
