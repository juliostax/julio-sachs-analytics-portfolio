### Problem

### Approach

This required analysis across:

* Revenue per pound
* Inventory requirements
* Cash-flow timing
* Fulfillment timing
* Production staffing
* Pricing strategy
* Channel economics

Key work included:

* Amazon revenue and unit-economics analysis
* 1P vs. 3P fulfillment transition planning
* Inventory and cash-flow modeling
* Production scheduling and daily fulfillment planning
* Labor-requirement analysis
* Subscription and reorder-rate analysis
* Marketplace performance tracking

### Result

| Metric                     |                                                                                                       Result |
| -------------------------- | -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------: |
| Amazon revenue per pound   |                                                            Increased from approximately **$13/lb to $22/lb** |
| Revenue continuity         |                                               Helped maintain overall revenue while improving unit economics |

## Case Study 2: Algorithmic MLB Betting Model

### Problem

The goal was to identify **late-market MLB underdog mispricing** using a model trained around lines available close to game time, rather than relying on early-market movement or opening-line closing value.

### Approach

I built a Python-based MLB betting simulation framework using:

* Probability estimation
* Expected-value logic
* Market-line comparison
* Real-world results tracking
* Stake sizing and bankroll exposure analysis
* Sample-size and variance review

The model focused on:

* Late-market lines within roughly one hour of game start
* Underdog-only market inefficiencies
* Expected-value decision logic
* Partial-season performance tracking
* ROI, net profit, sample size, and variance analysis

### Result

Tracked partial-season Betstamp performance:

| Metric                          |                                 Result |
| ------------------------------- | -------------------------------------: |
| MLB wagers tracked              |                                **227** |
| Approximate wager volume        |                             **$15.5K** |
| Net profit                      |                         **+$2,601.62** |
| ROI                             |                              **16.7%** |
| Average bet size                |                             **$67.80** |
| Net profit vs. average bet size | Approximately **38x average bet size** |

### Limitations

This was a partial-season tracked sample due to third-party app tracking limitations.

## Case Study 3: Crypto Strategy Dashboard & Dynamic Capital Allocation

### Problem

The goal was to build a trading-strategy research dashboard that could evaluate crypto-market opportunities across different yearly regimes, rather than assuming every year had the same number of tradable opportunities.

### Approach

I built a crypto strategy dashboard using historical simulation, market-data analysis, model scoring, dynamic allocation logic, and regime detection.

The system evaluates:

* Eligible coins
* Meta-strategy cumulative scores
* HIGH vs. LOW opportunity regimes
* Dynamic capital allocation
* NAV, peak NAV, and drawdown
* Trade count and average holding period
* Bootstrap events
* Active deployed capital versus idle preserved capital

In **HIGH-mode years**, the model allows broader deployment across up to 30 active coins.

In **LOW-mode years**, the model restricts active deployment to a small number of coins and reduces active capital exposure, preserving the remaining allocation for alternative use.

### Selected Historical Simulation Results

| Year / Mode             |       Starting Allocation |  Active Capital |                        Return |       Final NAV / Equity |     Peak NAV |                  Max Drawdown |  Trades |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------: | --------------: | ----------------------------: | -----------------------: | -----------: | ----------------------------: | ------: |
| 2023 HIGH mode          |                  $100,000 |        $100,000 |                    **+28.3%** |             **$128,272** | **$139,310** |                    **-13.4%** | **640** |
| 2024 HIGH mode          |                  $200,000 |        $200,000 |                    **+28.9%** |             **$257,884** | **$286,947** |                    **-17.9%** | **535** |
| 2025 LOW-mode safeguard | $200,000 input allocation | $2,000 deployed | **+280.5% on active capital** | **$7,609 active equity** |   **$7,631** | **-95.9% on active strategy** | **471** |

### 2025 LOW-Mode Safeguard Explanation

In 2025, early regime detection classified the year as a **LOW-mode environment**, meaning most eligible coins were modeled as poor opportunities according to the meta-strategy cumulative scores.

Instead of deploying the full $200,000 input allocation, the system automatically reduced active deployed capital to **$2,000** and limited exposure to up to **3 coins** at a time.

The purpose of this safeguard was to:

* Avoid forcing capital into a weak opportunity environment
* Preserve unused capital for alternative allocation
* Focus only on rare high-upside opportunities
* Maximize return on active deployed capital while limiting total portfolio exposure

The simulation produced approximately **$7,609 in active equity** from **$2,000 active capital**, while the remaining capital allocation was preserved rather than deployed into unfavorable market conditions.

### Limitations

These are historical simulation/backtest results, not live trading returns.

The purpose of the project is to demonstrate market-data analysis, strategy research, dashboarding, dynamic allocation logic, regime detection, and risk-aware simulation design.

### Skills Demonstrated

Python, trading simulation, market-data analysis, dashboarding, regime detection, capital allocation, drawdown analysis, backtesting, strategy research.
